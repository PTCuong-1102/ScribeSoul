The Silent Manuscript

The archive did not smell of dust or ancient paper, as Elias had expected. Instead, it smelled of ozone and the cold, metallic vacuum of deep space. Beneath the vaulted indigo ceiling of the Great Library, the silence was a physical weight, pressing against the eardrums with the persistence of a deep-sea diver's descent.

He placed his gloved hand upon the Obsidian Lectern. The surface-container-low shifted beneath his touch, a tonal change that rippled across the stone like ink dropped into a well of indigo light. There were no buttons here, no digital interfaces to crack. Only the intent of the scribe and the vast, waiting hunger of the Midnight Archivist.

"Tell me," Elias whispered, his breath frosting in the artificial chill. "Show me the records of the last voyage before the silence took the colony."

The air shimmered. A holographic scroll began to unfurl from the center of the room, casting long, violet shadows across the stacks. It wasn't a projection of light, but a carving of darkness—a absence of space that formed letters in a language lost to the inner worlds.
