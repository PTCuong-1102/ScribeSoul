The Silent Manuscript

The clock in the library didn't tick; it breathed. Each stroke of the mahogany pendulum seemed to inhale the dust of ancient folios and exhale a silence so profound it felt like a physical weight upon the shoulders. Julian sat at the desk, his fingers hovering above the parchment, afraid that the mere act of writing would shatter the fragile peace of the room.

He looked toward the window where the winter moon was hanging low over the manor's sprawling gardens.

Everything was precisely where it should be, yet something felt fundamentally altered. The shadows between the shelves were longer than they had been an hour ago, stretching like ink stains across the floor.

The antagonist had moved with a surgeon's precision. There were no broken locks, no ruffled papers. Only the void where the obsidian dagger had once rested.
